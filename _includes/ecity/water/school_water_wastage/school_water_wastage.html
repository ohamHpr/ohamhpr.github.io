Water Wastage in Schools

In the vast majority of cases, schools used to provide drinking water through a type of collective drinking trough equipped with a row of taps. Students, especially younger ones, would often neglect to close the taps resulting in significant water wastage. With tens of taps in each school and thousands of schools nationwide, this led to a considerable amount of water being wasted every school day.

Influencing policeis and measures was beyond the limitation of my young 19-year-old self, as was changing the standards in appliance manufacturing. Therefore, I focused on the observed pain points and inreffed that we require taps that operate similarly to push taps, without the need for physical contact and with easy installation.
By discussing with other school principals, I ensured that the inference closely matched the expectations.

Goal: Having the exact expectations in hand, considering whole initiative in mind, and thinking forward to the consequences, I created an comperhensive water management system with an effective prototype of smart taps mixed in innovation, simplisity, viablility, aesthetically pleasing, and full functionality. looks like a pipe with an eye (IR Sstem) in front of it towards users. 1. open the tap: just show up in front of it, and water flows for a specific time. 2. close the tap: just go away. 3. need more time: just wave your hand in front of it 4. need adjust volume: turn the eye. 5. need to control manully: just push the eye. 6. install in different angle: use its joint accessories. 7. smart blue light: turn on a shad if pass in front of it, turn on when opens.
